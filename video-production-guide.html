🎥 Video Production Guide

How to create high-quality Instagram Reels without expensive equipment

📱 Equipment You Already Have

Minimum Setup (What You Need)

  • iPhone or modern smartphone — Your phone camera is more than enough
  • Natural light or ring light — $20–50 on Amazon (Neewer, UBeesize)
  • Quiet room — Use your home office, bedroom, or rent a co-working space for filming days
  • Phone tripod — $15–30 on Amazon (adjustable, stable)
✅ Pro Tip: Film all 10 reels in one 2-hour session. Batch filming saves time and keeps your energy consistent across videos.

🎬 Background Recommendations

Your background should look professional but not sterile. Here are the best options for your coaching brand:

Option 1: Home Office / Workspace (RECOMMENDED)

PRO: Authentic, relatable, shows you're a working professional
SETUP: Clean desk, bookshelf behind you (business/leadership books visible), minimal clutter, good lighting

What to show: Laptop, notebook, whiteboard with frameworks, plants, professional but lived-in feel

Option 2: Neutral Wall with Branding

PRO: Clean, professional, keeps focus on you
SETUP: Plain wall (white, beige, navy), add ONE piece of wall art or "Andrew Tadros Coaching" text (vinyl decal or printed poster)

Cost: $10–20 for vinyl lettering on Amazon or local print shop in Cairo

Option 3: Co-working Space / Cafe (For Variety)

PRO: Dynamic, Egypt-local vibe, good for "Egypt founders" content
CON: Background noise (use lapel mic), inconsistent lighting

Best for: Reels 6, 8 (client transformation, Egypt-specific content)

Option 4: Outdoor (Cairo Landmarks)

PRO: Visually striking, shows you're Egypt-based
CON: Wind noise, lighting challenges, crowds

Use sparingly: 1–2 reels max for visual variety (Nile background, Cairo skyline at sunset)

⭐ BEST CHOICE FOR YOU: Start with Option 1 (Home Office) for 80% of your reels. It's authentic, controllable, and builds your personal brand as a working coach.

🎥 B-Roll Shots (Film These Once, Use Forever)

B-roll is footage that plays OVER your voice — so it's not just you talking. Film 15–20 clips in one session and reuse them across all reels.

Category 1: Desk & Work Environment

💻 Laptop Typing Close-Up

Shows you working. Hands only, or over-shoulder shot. Good for: any reel about productivity, systems, planning.

📝 Notebook Writing

Writing bullet points, frameworks, or client notes. Perfect for: delegation reel, priority matrix content.

📅 Calendar / Planner View

Google Calendar or physical planner. Great for: time management reels, weekly planning content.

📊 Whiteboard Writing

Write "Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3" or a quick framework. HIGHLY engaging visual. Use for: Reel 4 (delegation), Reel 9 (decision rule).

📞 Coffee Cup on Desk

Ambient, relatable. Works in any reel as a cutaway.

Category 2: App & Screen Recordings

📱 Phone App Screen Recording

Record a screen recording of your coaching app mockup (from programs.html). Scroll through it slowly. Use for: Reels discussing Premium/Elite tier coaching tools.

🖥️ Laptop Screen: Dashboard

Show your coaching dashboard, client notes, or Google Doc of a framework. Blurs can be added in editing for client privacy.

📊 Coaching Framework Slides

Create 3–4 slides in Canva (navy/gold brand colors) showing: 3-Tier Delegation, Priority Matrix, ADHD Toolkit. Record your screen as you scroll through them.

Category 3: Person-in-Action

🚶 Walking Shot (El Gouna / Red Sea)

Short walk clip outside. Shows confidence, location identity. Even 5 seconds works great as an intro/outro cutaway.

🤝 Zoom/Video Call Shot

Film yourself on a coaching call (no audio needed — just the visual of you on a call). Shows you actively coaching. Great social proof.

📱 Typing on Phone

Close-up of typing a message or checking calendar on phone. Universally relatable for busy leaders.

📍 El Gouna Advantage: The Red Sea / El Gouna marina and waterfront gives you a UNIQUE premium background that Cairo-based coaches don't have. Use it occasionally — it positions you as a premium, international coach while staying locally grounded.

💬 Captions & On-Screen Text (Step-by-Step)

Studies show 85% of Instagram Reels are watched with sound OFF. Captions are essential. Here's how to do them:

Option 1: Instagram's Auto-Captions (FREE — Start Here)

  • After uploading your reel, tap the sticker button
  • Select "Captions" — Instagram auto-generates subtitles
  • Style them: use large text, white with dark outline, or yellow
  • Review and fix any errors
  • Limitation: Basic styling, can look generic

Option 2: CapCut (FREE — Recommended)

CapCut App (iOS/Android — Free)

The best free editing tool for Reels. Key features for you:
• Auto-captions with word-by-word highlighting (very popular on Reels)
• Text overlays — animate your framework points appearing on screen
• B-roll merging — seamlessly cut between you speaking and B-roll
• Zoom effects — auto-cuts to make talking-head videos feel dynamic
• Arabic/English support
• Templates optimized for Reels 9:16 format

Option 3: Canva Video (For Branded Text Overlays)

Canva Pro or Free

Use for:
• Framework slides that animate onto screen (navy + gold brand)
• "Andrew Tadros Coaching" branded lower thirds
• Statistic callouts: "85% of leaders are burned out" appears as animated text
• Outro screens with CTA

🎨 Caption Style for Your Brand:
Use large bold white text with a semi-transparent dark background box. This maximizes readability without hiding your background. Font: Inter, Montserrat, or Poppins.

Caption placement: Center or lower-third (avoid the top — Instagram UI covers it).

📊 Visual Aids Per Reel (Exactly What to Show)

For each reel, here's the exact cut structure — when to show you, when to show B-roll, and what text to overlay.

Reel 1: Brand Introduction — "Who I Am & What I Do"

0–3s: YOU on camera, hook text overlay: "Most leaders in Egypt are running on empty"
3–8s: B-roll: typing on laptop
8–15s: YOU on camera: "smart, driven, and completely overwhelmed" — overlay: your name + title
15–22s: B-roll: whiteboard with "SYSTEMS" written, or screen of coaching framework
22–28s: YOU: "I don't offer fluff, I offer systems" — overlay: 3 bullet points appear
28–30s: YOU: "Let's build something that lasts" + outro card with "Link in bio"

Reel 2: ADHD Leaders — "Why ADHD Leaders Struggle"

0–3s: Text hook only (no face) over B-roll of sticky notes / messy planner
3–10s: YOU: "Most productivity advice for ADHD leaders is garbage"
10–18s: B-roll: screen recording of visual ADHD workflow/Kanban board
18–24s: YOU: "You need systems that match how your brain works" — bullet points animate in
24–30s: B-roll: coaching app screen scroll + CTA overlay

Reel 4: Delegation — "3-Tier Framework"

0–3s: Whiteboard shot: "Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3" written out
3–12s: YOU explaining each tier — return to whiteboard for each tier reveal
12–20s: B-roll: calendar audit on laptop screen
20–26s: YOU: "You should spend 80% of your time on Tier 3" — overlay: pie chart graphic
26–30s: CTA card

⚡ General Rule: Never stay on your face for more than 8–10 seconds without cutting to B-roll or a text overlay. This dramatically increases watch time.

📱 Coaching App Visual Integration

Your app (planned for Premium/Elite tiers) is a major differentiator. Here's how to show it on video even before it's built:

Now (Before App Launch):

  • Use the app mockup from your programs.html page as B-roll — record a screen capture of it
  • In CapCut, overlay the screen recording as a "picture-in-picture" insert while you speak
  • Caption: "Coming soon: The coaching app for Premium and Elite members"
  • This builds anticipation and exclusivity BEFORE launch

Once App is Live:

  • Dashboard B-roll: Scroll through the app dashboard slowly while you describe features
  • Session recap screen: Show the "weekly wins" or goal tracking view
  • Notification pop-up: Show an accountability reminder appearing on screen
  • Before/After overlay: Show "broken calendar" vs "structured coaching plan" side-by-side
💡 High-Impact Reel Idea: Film a 30-second "App Tour" reel:
  • Hook: "This is what our Premium coaching members get every week"
  • Screen record the app for 20 seconds
  • Close: "Elite tier — only 5 spots. Link in bio."
This creates urgency and showcases your premium positioning.

🎧 Audio Tips

  • Lapel mic: $15–25 on Amazon — plugs into iPhone headphone jack or USB-C. Dramatically improves audio quality.
  • Record in a carpeted room — hard floors echo. Use your bedroom if needed.
  • Background music: Add subtle music in CapCut (30–40% volume so your voice dominates). Choose instrumental / lo-fi tracks that don't distract.
  • No music on LinkedIn reposts — LinkedIn doesn't support audio the same way.

⏱️ Editing Workflow (CapCut, Step by Step)

  1. Import your main talking-head footage into CapCut
  2. Add auto-captions — review for errors (5 minutes)
  3. Cut out "um", "uh", and long pauses using the split tool
  4. Insert B-roll clips at the spots noted in your reel script
  5. Add text overlays for your framework points (one by one)
  6. Add your branded lower third (import from Canva as PNG)
  7. Add outro card at the end
  8. Add subtle background music (CapCut library or your own)
  9. Export at 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical, max quality)
  10. Upload to Instagram Reels — add caption from content.html
⏰ Time estimate per reel: 30–60 minutes to edit once you have all assets ready. After the first 2–3 reels, you'll get faster. Target: batch edit 5 reels in one Sunday afternoon.
